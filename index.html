The first Saudi state

The first Saudi state (1157 - 1233 AH / 1744 - 1818 AD) is a state established in the Arabian Peninsula, after the agreement between Prince Muhammad bin Saud and Sheikh Muhammad bin Abdul Wahhab, which resulted in the formation of a large political unit on the land of the Arabian Peninsula. among the many small political entities that were present in the Najd region. And it formed from it a political unit subject to one system, and this state remained in place until Ibrahim Pasha, the governor of Egypt, managed to overthrow it after his seizure of its capital, Diriyah, in 1233 AH / 1818. Four rulers succeeded in ruling the state during this period, and the first Saudi state, since its establishment, played a role that had far-reaching effects in the change that afflicted the Arabian Peninsula in various fields of life politically, economically, socially and culturally, as it unified not only the small Najdi entities, but also extended its influence over most parts of the country. The Arabian Peninsula, and threatened both Iraq and the Levant.

The second Saudi state

It is the state established by Turki bin Abdullah bin Muhammad Al Saud after the fall of the first Saudi state in the year 1233 AH - 1818 AD, at the hands of the forces of the Ottoman Eyalet of Egypt led by Ibrahim Muhammad Ali Pasha. During the years of his rule, Prince Turki bin Abdullah bin Muhammad Al Saud was able to take Riyadh as the capital of his kingdom instead of Diriyah, which expanded in a limited way unlike its predecessor, and internal conflict and wars damaged it, as the difference between the sons of Imam Faisal bin Turki bin Abdullah Al Saud caused In weakening the state and causing its downfall at the hands of the Emir of the Saudi state, Ali Hail, from the Al Rashid family, in the year 1309 AH corresponding to 1891 AD.

The third Saudi state

The third Saudi state is the heir of the two Saudi states: the first and the second. It was founded on (5 Shawwal 1319 AH - January 15, 1902 AD), by King Abdulaziz Al Saud, who managed to restore the city of Riyadh to establish the modern and contemporary Saudi state (the Kingdom of Saudi Arabia). The third Saudi state was known at the beginning of its era as the Emirate of Riyadh, and after the annexation of Al-Ahsa it was called the Emirate of Najd and Al-Ahsa. The name continued to exist until the declaration of the establishment of the Kingdom of Saudi Arabia in 1932.
